About Studio Ledger

Every template started as a fix for my own admin headache.

Studio Ledger is a one-person shop making the systems I wished existed during six years of freelance design work — now built for anyone running a studio of one.

Where it started

I spent more Sunday nights than I'd like to admit rebuilding the same invoice spreadsheet, because the last one had turned into a mess of broken formulas and mismatched tax columns.

Eventually I stopped rebuilding it and started documenting it properly — clear formulas, clean formatting, and no assumptions about how your business is supposed to run. Other freelancers started asking for a copy. That spreadsheet became The Ledger, and Studio Ledger grew from there.

Every template since has followed the same rule: it has to solve something I've personally been stuck on, not something that just sounds useful in a product description.

What guides the work

  • Made to be used, not admired

    No template ships until it's survived a real invoicing cycle or a real client handoff.

  • Priced like tools, not art

    A one-time price for a one-time build. No subscriptions for a spreadsheet.

  • Plain instructions, no jargon

    If a setup guide needs a glossary, the template needs a rewrite.
